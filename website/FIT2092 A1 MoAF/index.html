About Us

Welcome to the Museum of Alternative Facts (MoAF). We are an online museum documenting the rare and publicly unknown animals of the world. Our goal is to inform the public of exclusive and never before seen creatures, and to persuade the public to keep searching.

Leopeer

Aptenurnix spilogianus

↑

The leopeer is an rare hybrid who resembles the North American white-tailed deer and the African leopard. Because of its unusual combination between a predator and a prey, it possesses traits of both, being an omnivore. To compensate for its diet, it has developed pointed teeth, and sharpened horns for hunting.

Our sources note a pack of three were last seen in sub-Saharan Africa, but shortly fled after being discovered.

Use the buttons to hear the leopeer. Typically leopeers growl to scare off other predators, such as our field researcher who captured the noise and fled shortly after.

Albino Leopeer

Aptenurnix uncia

Also called snow leopeers, albino leopeers are an almost illogical sight. Albinism in leopeers is uncommon, and is said to give the viewer good fortune. Currently, there is only one known sighting of an albino leopeer, from an anonymous source in the Himalayas.

It has been speculated that the albinism trait is enhanced by the cold environment, the need to blend in with the white coloured atmosphere.

Albino leopeers are typically heavier than leopeers by around 15kg!

They also have bigger paws, and a thicker tail. These aid in its balance when moving through the snow.

Geckark

Tachygaius maxillus

The geckark is an aquatic critter found only in the deepest depths of the ocean. Geckarks are said to be the evolved descendants of a gecko trapped in the ocean, who evolved fins and a body similar to a shark.

However unlike a shark, the geckark has small, fine teeth used to eat krill and small fish. Hover over the geckark to view its skeletal anatomy!

Animal Hybrid Secrets

How hybrid animal parts can cure diseases

↑

These extremely uncommon hybrid animals are not only symbols of luck, but their scarcity can also be attributed to a publicly unknown characteristic.

Our team at the Museum of Alternative Facts have discovered that the hearts of hybrid animals shine with a spectrum of colours, rainbow coloured flesh we have dubbed 'Arcus Escam'. We have found that when ingested, Arcus Escam can fight against incurable diseases such as polio and influenza.

This discovery was made by MoAF head researcher Fayke Nhaime, who fed Arcus Escam to hundreds of patients, all of which recovered within days. However they reported symptoms of nausea, dizziness and deafness during recovery.
